## Why Publish Personality Assessment Results?

"What kind of person your colleague is" matters as much as their skills and experience.

Technical ability is visible on [my profile](/HomePage/en/profile/) and [project history](/HomePage/en/project/). But "how does this person think, communicate, and what causes them stress?" can't be read from a resume or portfolio.

That's why I'm publishing my personality assessment as **self-disclosure for future collaborators**.

## My Work Characteristics

### Communication Style

**Strengths:**
- Purpose-driven communication that eliminates waste
- Logically structuring complex problems for clear delivery
- Fact-based, not emotion-based decision making

**My note:** "Eliminating waste" doesn't mean skipping small talk or team dialogue. I **deliberately make space** for psychological safety conversations (1-on-1s, casual chat). I streamline **goal-oriented discussions**.

**Challenges:**
- Tendency to be too direct when others' opinions seem illogical
- Not naturally skilled at showing emotional empathy
- Minimizing communication with personality types I find difficult

**Working on it:** I'm studying "Strategic Communication" and "Adaptive Leadership." Being logically correct alone doesn't move people — a lesson learned through practice.

### Team Behavior

The assessment says my ideal environment offers "high autonomy and specialization" and "the ability to build complex systems independently."

Indeed, I'm most deeply engaged in personal projects ([YumeHashi](/HomePage/en/product/yumehashi/) / [Defrago](/HomePage/en/product/defrago/)) where I **design and implement everything from scratch**.

At the same time, I serve as a team leader in my current role. Balancing "I want to do everything myself" with "we need to deliver as a team" is my growth theme.

## In Closing

A personality assessment is just one lens — it doesn't define me completely. But **knowing and articulating your tendencies** is important for teamwork.

Just having the prior knowledge "this person is this type" significantly reduces communication friction. I hope this serves as a first step toward mutual understanding with future collaborators.
