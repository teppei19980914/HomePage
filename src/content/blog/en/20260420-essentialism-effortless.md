## "Doing Everything" Is the Same as "Doing Nothing"

How many items are on your task list right now?

At one point, I was endlessly adding tasks: work, side projects, certifications, reading, blogging. I believed doing everything would make me grow. Instead, everything became half-finished, and I couldn't focus on what truly mattered.

Two books gave me a clear answer:

- **Essentialism** = Determine **what** to do
- **Effortless** = Make **how** you do it easier

These are **two wheels of a car**. Even if you know what to do, you can't sustain it if the process is painful. Even if the process is easy, it's meaningless if you're doing the wrong thing.

---

## Essentialism — If It's Not "Absolute Yes," It's No

### The Pickle Jar Theory

Life is a jar with limited capacity.

| Order | Contents | Meaning |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1 | **Rocks** | What truly matters (dreams, family, health) |
| 2 | **Pebbles** | Important but not urgent (learning, exercise) |
| 3 | **Sand** | Trivial things (social media, notifications) |

**If you don't put rocks in first, sand fills the jar.** Most people fill their jar with sand and have no room for rocks.

### "The Courage to Say No" Is the Most Important Skill

The core of Essentialism: **anything you can't say "absolute yes" to is a no.**

"I guess I could" and "it would be rude to decline" — these are all nos. The book states clearly that **the courage and skill to say no is Essentialism's essential skill**.

### Sunk Cost Bias Distorts Judgment

| Bias | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Sunk cost bias** | "I've come this far" — continuing a failing project |
| **Endowment effect** | Excessive fear of letting go of what you have |

**The courage to stop what you're doing** — this is the hardest part of practicing Essentialism.

## Effortless — Connect "Fun" with "Must-Do"

### The 3-Layer Structure

| Layer | Question | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Effortless thinking** | What to think about | Release negativity, approach with positive emotions |
| **Effortless action** | What to do | Rapid small failures for accelerated learning |
| **Effortless systems** | How to systematize | Sleep routines, automation, checklists |

### Positive Emotions Are Powerful

The **broaden-and-build theory** shows that positive emotions widen perspective and spark new ideas. Negative emotions linger, consuming working memory.

The key: **connect "must-do" tasks with "enjoyable" activities**. People naturally do fun things first and procrastinate on obligations. Link the two, and obligations progress naturally.

### Sleep Is the Ultimate Effortless Action

Most people view sleep as an obligation to minimize. This is wrong.

The **10,000-hour rule** is misunderstood as "practice a lot and become a pro." In reality, **quality sleep is the prerequisite**. 10,000 hours of sleep-deprived practice yields dramatically lower results.

The book's effortless sleep system:

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| 90 min before bed | Take a bath |
| 60 min before bed | Turn off electronics |
| Same time daily | Go to sleep |

### Semantic Trees for Efficient Learning

Learn **core principles (trunk) first**, then attach new information as branches through trial and error.

For engineers: understanding design principles (MVC, reactive, declarative UI) behind frameworks enables dramatically faster adaptation to new ones, rather than memorizing APIs.

## Applying to Engineering

### Essential: Deciding "What to Build"

In the age of AI-driven development, the human's most important role is **deciding what to build**. This is pure Essentialism. Implementation can be delegated to AI, but judging "which feature truly matters" requires human judgment.

### Effortless: Systematizing Quality

Quality checks embedded in systems mean humans can forget and quality is still maintained:
- Test execution → **automated via Hooks**
- Security review → **automated via Agents**

## In Closing

Life's jar has limited capacity. Put rocks in first. Then make working on those rocks as easy as possible.

- **Essentialism**: If it's not "absolute yes," it's no. Have the courage to stop
- **Effortless**: Make obligations enjoyable. Systematize. Prioritize quality sleep

These two frameworks apply not just to engineering work, but to designing life itself.
